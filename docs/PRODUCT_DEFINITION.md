# Product Definition

## Product summary

Taily is an AI travel companion that helps users plan trips based on their travel style, evaluate how well an itinerary fits them, and refine the trip accordingly.

Unlike generic travel planning tools, Taily focuses on **trip fit**. Its goal is not just to generate a workable itinerary, but to help users understand whether a proposed trip actually feels right for them.

## Problem statement

Travel planning tools can generate itineraries, but they rarely help users answer a more personal question:

**Does this trip actually fit me?**

Users often struggle with questions like:
- Is this trip too packed for my preferred pace
- Is it too expensive for the kind of experience I want
- Is it too focused on famous landmarks instead of food, comfort, or exploration
- Why does this itinerary look reasonable, but still not feel right

As a result, users may get a trip plan, but still lack confidence in whether it matches their preferences.

Taily is designed to close that gap by combining trip generation with fit analysis and explainable refinement.

## Target users

Taily is initially designed for independent leisure travelers who:
- plan their own trips
- have personal preferences beyond generic travel templates
- want recommendations that feel tailored to them
- do not want to spend excessive time comparing routes, blogs, and planning tools

Primary early users:
- young to mid-career travelers
- users planning short to medium-length city or international trips
- users who care about how a trip feels, not just what it includes

## Core value proposition

Taily helps users move from **“a generated itinerary”** to **“a trip plan that feels right for me.”**

It creates value in three ways:

1. **Lightweight personalization**  
   Taily builds an initial travel profile through quick onboarding instead of requiring long forms or repeated manual filtering.

2. **Trip generation with user context**  
   Taily generates itineraries using both the current trip request and the user’s baseline travel style.

3. **Fit understanding and refinement**  
   Taily shows how well a trip matches the user’s preferences, explains where the fit is strong or weak, and helps the user quickly refine the trip in a direction that suits them better.

## Core product principles

### 1. Fit over generic optimization
The goal is not just to generate a logical itinerary, but one that feels personally suitable.

### 2. Natural input, structured understanding
Users should be able to express travel ideas naturally, while the system internally translates those inputs into a structured user and trip model.

### 3. Explainability matters
Users should understand why a trip matches or does not match them, rather than receiving an opaque recommendation.

### 4. Refinement should be lightweight
Adjusting a trip should feel easy and directional, not like restarting planning from scratch.

### 5. Personality is an enhancement layer
Traveler identity and companion-pet expression can make the product more memorable and emotionally resonant, but functional usefulness comes first.

## Why this product

Many travel tools focus on **what to do**.  
Taily focuses on **whether this version of the trip actually fits the traveler**.

This makes the product especially useful for users who are not just looking for ideas, but trying to make a more personal decision:
- Which version of the trip feels more like me
- Why this itinerary seems attractive but still feels off
- What direction I should change the trip in to make it better

Taily is built around that decision layer.

## Non-goals for MVP

The MVP will not attempt to be a full travel operating system.

It will not include:
- booking flows
- collaboration features
- social networking
- review aggregation from external platforms
- long-term memory beyond initial onboarding
- complex avatar generation
- real-time travel pricing optimization
- imported maps, PDFs, screenshots, or saved travel pins

The first goal is to validate the core **trip fit workflow**:
user profile → trip generation → fit analysis → refinement.
